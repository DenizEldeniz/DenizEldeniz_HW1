Games

I've loved playing games since my childhood. Playing online games with my friends in my free time is one of my biggest hobbies. Here are all the games I play:

Games I Actively Play

  • League of Legends (LoL)
    I've been playing for 9 years and I still can't quit! A team-based strategy and action-packed MOBA game. My friend Arda and I have been playing at least a few matches every evening since high school. Climbing ranked together is a whole different kind of joy. This game is not just a game for me — it's a lifestyle.
  • Valorant
    I've been playing for 3 years and I absolutely love it! An exciting tactical FPS that combines gunplay with character abilities. My friend Burak and I love queuing up as a 5-stack premade for competitive matches. We literally live for those clutch moments!
  • Albion Online
    I've been playing for 2 years. An open-world sandbox MMORPG full of economy, PvP, and crafting systems. Creating a guild with my friend Emre and doing PvP in the black zone gives us adrenaline-filled moments.
  • Minecraft
    I've been playing for 15 years — yes, really since childhood. The legendary sandbox game offering limitless creativity and adventure. I'll never forget the days we built massive castles in survival worlds.
  • DEVOUR
    I've only been playing for 1 year but it's already one of my favorite horror games! A 1-4 player co-op horror game. Last year my friend Mert and I started playing at 3 AM, we got so scared that we shut down the computer and went to sleep.

Survival Games

I have a special passion for survival games. Surviving in nature, gathering resources, and building bases is incredibly fun for me.

  • 7 Days to Die
    An open-world survival game with a zombie apocalypse theme. The horde attacks that come every 7 days are incredibly intense. My friends and I build fortresses and defend against zombies. The base building aspect keeps me glued to the screen for hours.
  • ARK: Survival Evolved
    A survival game in a world full of dinosaurs. Taming and riding dinosaurs is so much fun. I was incredibly excited when I tamed my first T-Rex. Building a tribe with my friends and fighting against other tribes is amazing.
  • ARK: Survival Of The Fittest
    The battle royale version of ARK. Everyone starts in the same arena, tames dinosaurs, and the last one standing wins. It's faster-paced and more competitive than regular ARK. Perfect for those who want quick action.
  • Erzurum
    A Turkish-made survival game! You try to survive in the harsh winter conditions of Erzurum. Chopping wood, hunting, staying warm... The fact that it's made by Turkish developers makes me extra proud. The atmosphere is really well crafted.
  • Green Hell
    A survival game set in the Amazon rainforest. One of the best survival games in terms of realism. Snakes, insects, diseases... Everything is trying to kill you. Playing co-op with my friends is very enjoyable but equally stressful.
  • Raft
    You start on a tiny raft in the middle of the ocean and try to survive by expanding your raft. My friends and I turned our raft into a massive ship. Escaping from the shark is a thrill on its own.
  • Sons Of The Forest
    The sequel to The Forest and just as terrifying. You try to survive on an island full of mutant creatures. Building a base with your AI companion Kelvin is very fun. When night falls, I don't dare to enter the caves.
  • The Forest
    After a plane crash, you try to survive in a forest inhabited by cannibals. When I first played it, I was genuinely terrified when the cannibals came at night. Cave explorations add another level of tension. This game is one of the best in the survival-horror genre.

The Witcher Series

I absolutely love The Witcher series. In terms of storytelling, world design, and character depth, it's the best RPG series I've ever played.

  • The Witcher: Enhanced Edition
    The first game of the series. Although the graphics are dated, the story and atmosphere are magnificent. Geralt's adventure begins here. Everyone who wants to play the series in order should start from this one.
  • The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings
    The second game, full of political intrigue and challenging combat mechanics. The fact that your choices in the story actually change the course of the game really impressed me. The graphic quality is a massive leap compared to the first game.
  • The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt
    I can say it's one of the best RPGs in gaming history. Its open world, side quests, story... Everything is perfect. The Blood and Wine DLC alone offers as much content as a full game. When I finished this game, I felt an emptiness inside — I was that attached to it.
